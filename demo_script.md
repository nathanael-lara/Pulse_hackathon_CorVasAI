# CorVas AI Demo Script

## 3-Minute Hackathon Demo

### 1. Problem Framing

Hi everyone, we built **CorVas AI**, a cardiac recovery companion designed to solve the participation paradox in cardiac rehab.

Maria leaves the hospital with a 12-week rehab plan that could dramatically improve her recovery, but like most patients, she faces emotional, logistical, and behavioral barriers once she gets home.

Our goal was to build something that does more than track steps. We wanted to create a system that helps Maria understand her care, stay engaged through rehab, and get support before small drop-offs become dangerous setbacks.

---

### 2. Voice of Care: The Method of Care

First, we’ll show our method of care.

Maria has just finished a walk. In CorVas AI, her rehab activity is logged, her progress updates, and the AI responds with supportive reinforcement tied to her physician-guided plan.

We can show the app recognizing that she completed today’s activity, confirming that she stayed in a safe range, and giving her simple encouragement that feels personal instead of generic.

The key here is that the AI is not inventing medical advice. It stays grounded in the doctor’s plan, the visit transcript, and the rehab schedule already assigned to her.

---

### 3. Voice of Care: The Wall

Now we’ll show how we solve the urge to quit treatment.

Maria is tired, anxious, and she skipped a rehab session. This is where most patients disengage.

In CorVas AI, the system detects the missed rehab step, checks medication adherence and recovery context, and responds with an emotionally supportive intervention.

Instead of blame, the AI explains what happened in plain language, encourages the next safest step, and escalates when needed. If warning signs stack up, the app can surface a red-flag path to the care team or family support network.

This is important because recovery failure is often not one dramatic event. It is small moments of uncertainty, fear, and missed follow-through.

---

### 4. The Hook: Why Maria Stays Engaged at Week 8

Our engagement hook is **supportive recovery accountability**.

By Week 8, the original hospital scare has faded, so CorVas AI keeps Maria engaged through three things:

- a structured 12-week rehab plan she can review week by week
- family and community support, including rides and care matching
- encouragement and reminders that are tied to her actual progress, not generic notifications

This makes the experience feel less like a tracker and more like a care companion.

The system keeps showing Maria what she has completed, what is next, and who is in her corner if she starts to fall behind.

---

### 5. The Product Walkthrough

On the web app and iPhone app, Maria can move through the full recovery journey:

- **Visit** for live doctor explanations, pre-visit prep, and post-visit follow-up
- **Medications** for reminders, adherence tracking, and escalation if doses are repeatedly missed
- **Rehab Program** for the interactive 12-week recovery plan, progress history, and feedback
- **Documents** for plain-language summaries of labs, prescriptions, and visit materials
- **Family & Support** for recovery companions, transport help, and nearby care matching

We intentionally designed this to feel calm, accessible, and elder-friendly rather than like a hospital dashboard.

---

### 6. The Blueprint

Here is the architecture behind it.

Wearable-style data like steps and heart rate, along with medication adherence, rehab progress, and visit transcripts, flow into CorVas AI.

The AI layer uses that context to:

- explain doctor guidance in plain language
- generate grounded follow-up support
- personalize encouragement and reminders
- detect red-flag patterns such as repeated missed medication, skipped rehab, or concerning symptoms

When those red flags appear, the system escalates to a human layer: family support, matched providers, or the care team.

So the architecture is not just patient-to-AI. It is patient, context, AI support, and human escalation in one loop.

---

### 7. Closing

CorVas AI helps patients not only understand what their doctor said, but actually follow through on recovery at home.

We built a system that combines clear AI communication, engagement through the full 12-week journey, and a real support network around the patient.

That is how we turn cardiac rehab from a clinical mandate into a journey a patient is more likely to finish.
